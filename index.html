About Me

Three years ago, a single misconfigured load balancer took down checkout for 200,000 customers during a flash sale. I was the one paged at 2 AM, and by morning I had not only fixed the issue but built the automated health check that made sure it never happened again.

That incident captures how I work. I run reliability for services at Chewy that handle millions of requests every day. I lead cross-functional initiatives to cut incidents, drive platform standards, and still write Terraform and debug production with my own hands.

Before Chewy, I helped Best Buy scale their container platform across EKS and built CI/CD pipelines from scratch at ValourUSA. Each role taught me that the best infrastructure is the kind nobody notices because it just works.

Right now I'm deep into AIOps — I built a RAG-based assistant that reviews HAProxy pull requests against internal standards. I believe the next shift in operations is AI-assisted incident triage, and I want to be the one building it.

What I'm Thinking About

AIOps is the next SRE multiplier

I built a RAG-based assistant that reviews HAProxy pull requests against our internal standards and flags config risks before a human even looks at the diff. The next step is AI-assisted incident triage — correlating alerts, logs, and recent deploys to surface probable root causes in seconds instead of minutes. I think the SRE who learns to build these tools will be worth five who don't.

Platform engineering will replace traditional ops

The "ticket-driven ops team" model is dying. I've seen it firsthand: teams that build internal platforms with self-service guardrails ship faster and page less than teams that gate every change through an ops review. The winning pattern is golden paths with escape hatches, not gatekeeping.

Observability needs a standard, not more dashboards

Most teams I've worked with have three monitoring tools and still can't answer "why is this slow?" in under ten minutes. OpenTelemetry is the right bet — one instrumentation layer, any backend. I'm migrating services to OTel now because vendor-neutral telemetry is the only way to keep observability costs from eating your infrastructure budget.

Key Technical Achievements

Observability Platform Migration

Problem: 100+ ECS services relied on Splunk for monitoring, with costs growing and no unified view across the stack.

Approach: I migrated all services to Dynatrace with Terraform-managed agent deployments, replacing fragmented tooling with a single observability platform.

Result: Unified monitoring across every service, reduced observability costs by 30%, and cut mean-time-to-detect from 12 minutes to under 3.

AWS Cost Optimization

Problem: AWS spend was climbing quarter over quarter with no visibility into what was driving costs — CloudWatch alone cost ~$50k/quarter.

Approach: I ran a Datadog-driven utilization audit, rightsized instances, killed idle resources, and re-architected log routing with Lambda-based automation and smarter retention policies.

Result: Cut monthly AWS spend by 25% overall and reduced CloudWatch costs from ~$50k/quarter to ~$90k/year.

  • Datadog
  • AWS Lambda

  • Terraform

Zero-Touch HAProxy Deployment

Problem: HAProxy config changes required manual SSH into EC2 instances, making every rollout slow and error-prone.

Approach: I built a fully automated pipeline with GitHub Actions that validates configs, deploys to EC2, and handles cookie-based sticky sessions for traffic migration.

Result: Eliminated all manual deployment steps — rollouts went from 45 minutes of hands-on work to a single PR merge.

  • HAProxy
  • GitHub Actions
  • AWS EC2
  • Terraform

ECS to EKS Migration

Problem: Production workloads on ECS had inconsistent deployment patterns and limited orchestration capabilities as the team scaled.

Approach: I planned a phased migration to EKS with Helm charts, using traffic shifting to move services one at a time without downtime.

Result: Migrated all production workloads with zero downtime, standardized deployments across 30+ services, and reduced config drift between environments.

  • Kubernetes
  • Amazon EKS
  • Helm
  • Terraform
